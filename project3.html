Project Overview

AI-Driven Precision Farming for Mangoes

Problem: Traditional mango farming relies heavily on manual crop inspection, which is time-consuming, prone to errors, and inevitably leads to yield losses and resource wastage.

Scope: The project delivers a unified, AI-driven precision agriculture software system that automates the entire mango cultivation life cycle. It encompasses mango variety classification, disease detection, non-destructive ripeness prediction, and fruit grading according to UN/ECE standards.

Technical Stack

RepVGG-A1 ConvNeXt-Tiny CatBoost & SHAP CoaT Transformer Streamlit

Methodology

The project systematically replaces manual guesswork with automated, explainable machine learning models designed to optimize evaluation metrics across all agricultural stages.

Pipeline Stages

  • Robust Preprocessing: Applies HSV color masking, grayscale edge detection, circularity filters, and Hough Transforms to isolate fruit and eliminate outdoor background noise.
  • Variety Classification: Uses the RepVGG-A1 CNN, selected for its structural re-parametrization, offering high efficiency for real-time deployment.
  • Disease Detection: ConvNeXt-Tiny paired with Layer-CAM identifies leaf diseases and generates compact, highly accurate visual heatmaps with exceptional computational runtime.
  • Ripeness Prediction: A CatBoost regression model (paired with SHAP) predicts internal chemical attributes (sweetness/acidity) non-destructively using external tabular data like days after flowering and RGB values.
  • Fruit Grading: A hybrid CoaT-Lite Small Transformer + Random Forest + LIME model categorizes fruits into UN/ECE grades by fusing advanced semantic features with low-level texture details.

Outcomes & Results

The integrated system proved highly effective across all modules when evaluated against baselines and alternative algorithms:

  • Classification Efficiency: RepVGG-A1 achieved a peak accuracy of 99.3%, matching heavier models while remaining lightweight.
  • Superior Disease Localization: Layer-CAM vastly outperformed Grad-CAM and Eigen-CAM, achieving an average Intersection over Union (IoU) overlap of 0.93 and a runtime of 0.24 seconds.
  • Robust Ripeness Accuracy: CatBoost proved the most reliable for maturity prediction, achieving the highest R2 score (0.7482 for Total Soluble Solids) and the lowest Mean Absolute Error.
  • Grading Reliability: The grading model achieved an 89% validation accuracy and a Cohen's Kappa score of 0.794.
  • Accessible Deployment: Successfully unified all capabilities into a transparent, single Streamlit web application providing farmers with a personalized decision-support dashboard.

Project Overview

Energy Optimized AGV Path Planning and Fleet Management

Problem: Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs) are heavily utilized in manufacturing and logistics but face a significant challenge regarding energy consumption, typically using 0.22 kWh/ton-km per day. Traditional path planning focuses solely on minimizing distance and time, ignoring the high operational costs and environmental impact of battery depletion.

Scope: The project aims to develop a multi-objective path planning system that integrates traditional navigation with comprehensive energy requirement modeling and battery charging strategies to optimize both efficiency and sustainability in grid environments.

Technical Stack

Methodology

The project optimizes AGV navigation by shifting the traditional focus from shortest distance to minimum energy consumption, integrating battery charging logistics directly into the routing algorithm.

Algorithmic Enhancements

  • Energy-Optimized A* Algorithm: The standard A* algorithm was modified to use an energy-based cost function. The actual cost from the start to a node ($g(n)$) is calculated as the total energy consumed across different AGV states (driving, control, load handling), while the heuristic ($h(n)$) estimates the remaining energy to the goal.
  • Movement Penalties: The system mathematically penalizes high-speed acceleration to minimize energy spikes, and discourages excessive direction changes to lower control energy costs.
  • Path Smoothing: Cubic spline interpolation is applied to smooth out the optimal trajectory, removing sharp, energy-draining zigzag turns.
  • Battery Management Integration: The algorithm dynamically monitors battery levels during computation. If energy drops below a threshold, it optimizes the selection of a charging station using strategies like NCS (closest station) or MDCS (minimum delay).

Outcomes & Results

The optimized model was tested against standard pathfinding approaches, yielding the following results:

  • Massive Energy Savings: Achieved an Energy Efficiency Ratio (EER) of up to 1.66, resulting in total energy consumption savings of 39.74% compared to traditional A*.
  • Environmental Impact: The optimized routing translates directly to sustainability improvements, saving up to 26.74 kg of CO2 emissions in simulated scenarios.
  • Movement Efficiency: Significantly streamlined physical movement, reducing sharp turns from 32 down to just 2, and cutting overall path length from 76.64 to 59.76.
  • Computational Speed: Reduced path computation time from 0.0039 seconds to 0.0015 seconds, maintaining a manageable time complexity of $O(b^d)$ despite the added energy calculations.

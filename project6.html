Project Overview

6. Hierarchical Optimization Technique for Placement of Visual Landmarks

The core of this repository is a novel three-stage hierarchical optimization algorithm for robot navigation. Rather than uniformly distributing tags (which causes visual clutter and infrastructure redundancy), the algorithm mathematically calculates the optimal placement of Functional, Junction, and Corridor tags. The system successfully maximizes localization availability while drastically reducing the required infrastructure, validated across MATLAB, Webots, and real-world hardware.

Methodology

The tag placement strategy follows a structured, visibility-aware optimization framework. We began with Environment Definition & Feasibility Analysis, extracting corridor geometry and using camera intrinsics alongside pixel detection thresholds to compute the maximum detection distance. Next, a Visibility Matrix was generated to map candidate tags against sampled robot poses, checking for distance, bearing angle, and occlusion constraints.

Multi-Stage Tag Placement

  • Stage 1 (Functional Tags): Prioritizes high-value task areas, placing markers specifically at doorways and functional zones.
  • Stage 2 (Junction Tags): Secures error-prone intersections (L- and T-junctions) by placing outer and inner tags to ensure continuous visibility during heading changes.
  • Stage 3 (Corridor Tags): Fills remaining straight-line gaps using a greedy iterative algorithm that penalizes redundancy and minimizes blackout regions.

Outcome & Results

The proposed hierarchical optimization technique yielded significant improvements over manual and uniform placement strategies:

  • Localization Availability: Achieved 94.2% availability in simulated environments and 92% - 98.6% in real-time hardware validation.
  • Infrastructure Reduction: Reduced total marker infrastructure by 82% (from 178 candidate tags down to just 32 optimized tags).
  • Reduced Blackout: Minimized maximum blackout lengths to just 0.3 meters in straight corridors.
  • Consistent Visibility: Maintained a mean visibility of 1.011 tags per frame across the entire trajectory.
  • Hardware Reliability: Validation on the Yahboom Jetson bot confirmed the algorithm's robustness, matching theoretical predictions at varying operational speeds.
